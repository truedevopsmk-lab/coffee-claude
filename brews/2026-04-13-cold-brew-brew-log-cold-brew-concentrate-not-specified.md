## ⚖️ Brew Recipe (Cold Brew Concentrate)

* **Coffee:** 100 g
* **Water:** 1000 ml
* **Ratio:** 1:10
* **Grind Size:** Coarse (French press range, ~25–30 clicks)
* **Water Temperature:** Room temperature
* **Brew Time:** 12–18 hours

---

## ⏱ Brew Steps (Standard Immersion)

1. Grind coffee to a coarse consistency.
2. Add coffee grounds to the brewing vessel.
3. Pour water evenly to fully saturate all grounds.
4. Stir gently to ensure no dry pockets remain.
5. Cover and let steep at room temperature for 12–18 hours.
6. After steeping, filter using a paper filter or fine mesh.
7. Transfer the concentrate to a server or bottle for storage.

## 👅 Cup Profile & Notes

* **Aroma:** Smooth, low intensity
* **Taste:** Chocolatey, mellow sweetness
* **Acidity:** Very low
* **Body:** Heavy, rounded
* **Finish:** Clean, slightly lingering

This produces a versatile concentrate with muted acidity and a smooth, rounded profile. Works well as a base for dilution or milk-based drinks. The longer steep leans toward deeper chocolate and syrupy notes.

---

## 🔧 Adjustment Notes

* Shorten steep to 12 hrs for more brightness
* Extend to 18 hrs for heavier body and sweetness
* Dilute 1:1 with water or milk depending on preference

## 📝 Journal Thoughts

This is a solid base recipe for a reliable cold brew concentrate. The 1:10 ratio gives enough strength to experiment with different dilutions and additions without losing character.

What stands out is the flexibility — it works equally well with tonic, coconut water, or milk. The low acidity makes it easy to drink even in larger quantities, especially in warmer weather.

The cream + vanilla syrup variation sounds like a dessert-style drink worth trying. Might explore dialing grind size slightly finer next time to see if extraction improves without introducing bitterness.
